Four-Component Detection Engine

NeuroShield combines multiple adversarial analysis strategies to identify trojan behaviour with high precision and low false-positive rate.

⚡ COMPONENT 01
White-Box Perturbation

Applies FGSM and I-FGSM gradient attacks directly on the model to measure perturbation magnitude (δ), confidence drops, and gradient norms — key indicators of backdoor sensitivity.

◎ COMPONENT 02
Black-Box Boundary Attack

Without gradient access, employs random noise injection and the HopSkipJump boundary-walking algorithm to estimate decision-surface distances and attack success rates.

◈ COMPONENT 03
22-D Feature Profiling

Aggregates outputs from both attack strategies into a rich 22-dimensional feature vector capturing activation statistics, layer divergence, gradient magnitudes, and boundary characteristics.

⬡ COMPONENT 04
Behaviour Analyser & Outlier Detector

Feeds the feature vector through a Gaussian stability analyser and scikit-learn Isolation Forest classifier to produce a final anomaly score and binary verdict with certified robustness radius.

WORKFLOW

Analysis Pipeline

Each scan follows a rigorous four-stage pipeline executed in sequence for reproducible, explainable results.

01
Load & Preprocess

Image is normalised to the configured resolution (32×32, 64×64, or 224×224) and converted to a PyTorch tensor. Model weights are loaded from the selected source (demo SimpleConvNet or user-uploaded .pt / .pth file).

PYTORCH TORCHVISION NORMALIZATION
02
White-Box Adversarial Attack

With full gradient access, FGSM computes a single-step perturbation δ = ε · sign(∇ₓ L). I-FGSM refines it over multiple iterations. Perturbation magnitude, gradient norms, and prediction confidence are recorded.

FGSM I-FGSM GRADIENT ANALYSIS
03
Black-Box Boundary Estimation

Without internal model access, HopSkipJump uses binary-search binary decisions to walk the decision boundary and estimate minimal perturbation distance. Attack success rates are measured across noise levels.

HOPSKIPJUMP RANDOM NOISE BOUNDARY WALK
04
Classification & Verdict

The aggregated 22-D feature vector is scored by a Gaussian stability model (Z-score deviation) and an Isolation Forest trained on clean-model distributions. The combined anomaly score determines the final verdict.
